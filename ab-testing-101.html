A/B Testing 101 —
the complete guide.

Everything you need to understand, plan, and run experiments that actually produce reliable results. Written by V.

A/B testing is not about guessing which button colour converts better. Done properly, it is a systematic scientific method for understanding human behaviour at scale — and using that understanding to improve every metric that matters to your business.

Most companies run tests wrong. They peek at results early. They call winners at 60% confidence. They test one page while ignoring the three pages before it that actually determine intent. The result: a programme that feels busy but delivers nothing.

This hub will show you how to do it right — from the statistics to the strategy to the culture.

Core concepts

The building blocks every experimenter must know

01
The Hypothesis

Every test starts with a hypothesis, not an idea. Format: "Because we observed [data], we believe that changing [element] for [audience] will result in [outcome] because [rationale]." If you can't fill in every blank, you're not ready to test.

02
Statistical Significance

Significance tells you the probability your result is real, not random noise. 95% confidence means only a 5% chance the result is a fluke. Never call a winner below 95%.

03
Sample Size

Underpowered tests are the most common mistake. Calculate visitors needed per variant based on baseline conversion rate and minimum detectable effect before launching.

04
The ICE Score

Impact, Confidence, Ease — score each test idea 1–10 on each, average the three, prioritise by highest total. Imperfect but fast.

05
Primary vs Secondary Metrics

Your primary metric is what you're optimising for — revenue per visitor, leads, add-to-cart. Never optimise a secondary metric at its expense.

06
Novelty Effect

Changing a familiar page causes a temporary lift from curiosity alone. Run tests a minimum of two business cycles before reading results.
